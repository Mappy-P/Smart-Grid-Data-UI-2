Data Optimization

Smart grid data

Objective

The main goal of the algorithm is trying to find an efficient and green way to charge cars at EnergyVille making use of the solar energy provided by the solar panels installed at EnergyVille.

Input

The inputs of the algorithm consists of predicted data of the green solar energy and consumption energy of the company. It’s the excess energy, solar energy after consumption, that will be used to charge the cars. Also the predicted Belpex (Belgian Power Exchange) prices will be used, so we know when to buy energy as cheap as possible.

The Algorithm

In General

The energy data used by the algorithm is given per quarter of an hour. Therefore the charging scheme also works per quarter of an hour. To charge the cars as efficient as possible the algorithm will need to make use of the available solar energy as much as possible and buy energy when the Belpex prices are at it’s lowest. So the algorithm can be split into two loops.

First Loop

The first loop will only use available solar energy to charge the cars, this loop will make a partial charging scheme. To do this the loop will iterate true each car and charge them with the available solar energy. Sometimes there will be excess energy and this will be injected into the net, making a small profit. Most of the time the cars aren’t completely charged and that’s were the second loop comes in to play.

Second Loop

Now that the partial scheme is made some cars still need to be charged at full capacity, so some extra energy needs to be bought. Thanks to machine learning we have an idea of when the Belpex prices will be at their lowest and we know when to buy energy and charge the cars who still need to be charged.

Assumptions

To make an efficient and fast running algorithm certain assumptions are made. We assume Belpex prices aren’t going to be negative meaning you receive money when buying from the net. In this algorithm it’s not going to be the most optimal when prices are negative because it thinks it’s best to charge cars with solar energy instead of buying energy.

Optimization of the Algorithm

The algorithm isn't the most most optimal because of certain assumptions to make it run faster and efficient. But still after certain assumptions the algorithm is great aproximation of the most optimal.
